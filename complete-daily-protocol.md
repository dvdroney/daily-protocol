## WAKE-UP BLOCK

### Buteyko Nose-Unblocking Exercise (1-2 min)

Do this first thing. You can't mew or do the oral exercises later if you can't breathe through your nose.

1. **Sit upright.** Straight-backed chair or standing against a wall.
2. **Small, gentle breath in through your nose** (2 seconds). If totally blocked, tiny sip through the corner of your mouth.
3. **Small, gentle breath out through your nose** (3 seconds).
4. **Pinch your nose shut.** Mouth closed.
5. **Gently nod your head up and down** while holding. Keep going until you feel a moderate-to-strong urge to breathe (~70% of max hold).
6. **Release and breathe IN through your nose only.** Keep it calm — resist gulping air. Slow, relaxed breathing for 30-60 seconds.
7. **Repeat up to 5 times** with ~1 minute of nasal breathing between rounds.

Also consider adding a **nasal saline rinse** (NeilMed or neti pot) during this window — great for Iowa allergies and Hashimoto's-related congestion.

## POST-WORKOUT — SHOWER + MORNING ROUTINE

### Shower (~5-7 min)

Shower immediately after your run/workout. Use **lukewarm water** — hot water strips oils from your already-dry Hashimoto's skin and scalp.

**Hair washing schedule:**
- **Monday & Friday:** Use CeraVe Anti-Dandruff Hydrating Shampoo. Lather into scalp, then **leave it on for 3-5 minutes** while you wash your body. The pyrithione zinc needs contact time. Rinse, follow with conditioner.
- **Wednesday:** Use **Nizoral A-D (1% ketoconazole)** instead. Same technique — lather, leave on 3-5 min. Ketoconazole has a mild anti-androgenic effect on the scalp and studies show hair density improvements comparable to minoxidil 2%.
- **Other days:** Either skip shampooing entirely (just rinse with water) or use a gentle moisturizing shampoo like CeraVe Gentle Hydrating Shampoo. Over-washing strips what little natural oil your thyroid-slowed scalp produces.

**In the shower or right after — Scalp massage (2 min):**
The warm water loosens the scalp tissue, making this an ideal time.

**Technique 1: Circular Kneading (60 sec)**
- All 10 fingertip pads on scalp. Press firmly — scalp skin should **move over the bone**, creating folds. Not just sliding over hair.
- Small, slow circular motions (~1-inch circles). Start at front hairline, work back to crown, then sides, then nape.
- Extra time on crown/thinning area.

**Technique 2: Pinch and Lift (30 sec)**
- Grab sections of scalp between fingertips and palm heel. Squeeze together to form a crease, release. Work across the full scalp in a grid.

**Technique 3: Scalp Shake (30 sec)**
- Both hands flat on sides of head. Grip firmly and shake rapidly — scalp should slide over the skull. Move hand positions around.

### Morning Facial Exercise Block (10 min)

**Exercise 1 — Chin Tuck + Posture Set (2 min)**
1. Back flat against a wall. Feet ~6 inches out. Butt, upper back, and back of head all touching.
2. Shoulders rolled back and down.
3. Pull chin straight back toward wall — double chin. Don't tilt head.
4. Hold 5 seconds. Release 2 seconds. **15 reps.**

**Exercise 2 — Mewing Posture Set (2 min)**
1. Swallow. At the peak, notice tongue suctioned to palate — that's the target position.
2. Hold. Key points: get the **back third of tongue up** (most important/hardest part); gentle suction, not forceful pressing; teeth lightly touching or barely apart; jaw muscles relaxed; lips sealed.
3. Practice 5-6 intentional swallows during this time. Each one resets correct position.
4. Set phone reminders throughout the day for the first few weeks.

**Exercise 3 — Thumbpulling (4 min)**
- Wash hands. Stand in chin-tuck position (posture is critical).
- Place **both thumbs** on hard palate, pads facing up, just behind front teeth on either side of the midline ridge.
- Apply **steady upward pressure** — pressing UP, not outward. Lift first, let expansion happen naturally.
- Hold **30 seconds.** Breathe through nose. Maintain posture.
- Rest 10 seconds. **5 reps.**
- **Alternate days:** Lateral thumbpull — one thumb on each side, gentle outward-and-upward pressure. Same timing.
- **Never press on teeth** — only palate bone. No pain = correct position.

**Exercise 4 — Tongue Strengthening (2 min)**

*Tongue push-ups:*
Press entire tongue (front, middle, back) hard against palate. Max force. Hold 10 seconds. Rest 5 seconds. **8 reps.**

*Or alternate with tongue chewing (replaces gum):*
Tongue tip behind front teeth on palate. Press middle/back of tongue up firmly. Make chewing/kneading motion with tongue against palate. 60 seconds on, 15 seconds rest, **2 rounds.**

## MID-DAY / LUNCH

| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Klean Athlete Klean Iron** | 1 tablet (27 mg) | Take **away from dairy, coffee, and calcium.** Iron competes with calcium for absorption. Coffee/tea tannins also block iron. |

**Timing logic:** Iron is separated from your morning calcium-rich foods and coffee, and from your evening zinc. Iron and zinc compete for the same transporter, so spacing them apart maximizes absorption of both.

**Hashimoto's note:** Autoimmune thyroid patients are at higher risk for iron deficiency. Low iron also exacerbates hair loss — so this supplement is pulling double duty for you. Worth getting ferritin levels checked periodically (aim for ferritin 50-100+ for hair health, not just "in range").

**Runner's note:** Runners lose iron through foot-strike hemolysis, sweat, and GI losses. The 27 mg dose is appropriate for a male runner. If you ever feel unusually fatigued or your pace drops for no clear reason, get ferritin checked — runners can deplete iron faster than it's replaced.

## EVENING ROUTINE BLOCK (~8:30-9:30 PM)

### Evening Skincare — No Shower (5 min)

**No shower in the evening.** You're applying rosemary oil to your scalp to leave in overnight, and hot water/steam increases retinol irritation. Micellar water + gentle cleanser is all you need to remove sunscreen and grime.

**Step 1 — Double cleanse: micellar water (1 min)**
Removes sunscreen and daily grime. Simple Micellar Water on cotton pad.

**Step 2 — Gentle cleanser (1 min)**
Same CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser.

**Step 3 — Retinol (30 sec)**
Pea-sized amount to **dry** face. Start every other night for 2-3 weeks. Expect some dryness/peeling initially.

**Step 4 — Heavy moisturizer (30 sec)**
Layer on after retinol absorbs for a minute. Slugging (thin Aquaphor layer) on really dry winter nights.

**Step 5 — Rosemary oil scalp treatment (2 min)**
3-5 drops rosemary essential oil in ~1 tbsp carrier oil (jojoba or castor). Apply to crown/thinning areas. Massage using circular kneading and pinch-and-lift techniques. Leave in overnight. Towel on pillow.

### Evening Facial Exercise Block (10 min)

**Exercise 1 — Mewing Re-set (1 min)**
Conscious check-in. Full tongue suction on palate. 5-6 intentional swallows.

**Exercise 2 — Lying Chin Retractions (2 min)**
Lie face-up on floor, no pillow. Press back of head into floor while tucking chin. Hold 5 sec. **15 reps.**

**Exercise 3 — Thumbpulling Session 2 (4 min)**
Same as morning. 5 reps of 30-second holds. Strict posture. (After 8 weeks, you can drop to 1x/day.)

**Exercise 4 — Facial Massage / Lymphatic Drainage (3 min)**
Using jojoba oil:
1. **Forehead:** Center → sweep outward to temples. 5 passes.
2. **Cheeks:** Nose sides → sweep along cheekbones to ears. 5 passes.
3. **Jawline:** Chin center → sweep along jaw to below ears. 5 passes.
4. **Neck:** Stroke downward from below ears to collarbone. 5 passes each side.
5. **Sinus relief:** Small circles along brow, down nose sides, under eyes, over cheekbones.

### Final Step — Mouth Tape

Apply a small piece of tape (Hostage Tape or 3M Micropore) over the center of your lips. This trains nasal breathing during sleep and reinforces mewing posture overnight. Start with just center-lip taping so you can still open if needed.

Your dandruff shampoo slots into your **post-workout shower** (see Shower section above). Here's the full detail on the approach:

Your dry, white-flake, scalp-only, year-round dandruff is classic **Hashimoto's dry scalp** — your thyroid is slowing skin cell turnover and reducing oil production, so the scalp gets dry and sheds. This is simpler to treat than fungal seborrheic dermatitis and responds well to hydration-focused anti-dandruff products.

Use 2-3x per week. Contains 1% pyrithione zinc (anti-flake active) plus ceramides, niacinamide, and hyaluronic acid — the same skin-barrier ingredients as the CeraVe face products you're already using. Sulfate-free and fragrance-free, so it won't strip your already-dry scalp.

**How to use it properly:**
1. Wet hair. Apply shampoo and massage into scalp.

**Iron + hair loss:** With Hashimoto's + running + hair thinning, iron status is a major variable. Get ferritin checked — aim for 50-100+, not just "in range." Low ferritin is one of the most common and fixable causes of hair thinning.

**Congestion is the keystone:** If you can't nasal breathe, you can't mew, you mouth breathe at night, sleep quality drops, recovery suffers, and inflammation rises. Solving congestion (Buteyko, saline, mouth tape, thyroid optimization, possible dairy reduction) unlocks everything else.

## FUTURE INTERVENTIONS — Consider After 6 Months

If the current stack (rosemary oil, microneedling, ketoconazole, saw palmetto, scalp massage, selenium, iron optimization) hasn't produced visible results by month 6, consider adding:

**Minoxidil 5% (Rogaine) — topical, OTC**
The gold standard for non-prescription hair regrowth, FDA-approved with decades of clinical trial data. Works through vasodilation — increases blood flow to follicles and prolongs the growth phase. Completely different mechanism from everything else in this protocol, so it stacks well. Apply to crown/thinning areas 1-2x daily. No interaction with levothyroxine.

Caveats:
- Requires **daily use indefinitely** — stopping causes loss of gains within a few months
- May cause initial "shedding phase" (weeks 2-8) as weak hairs are pushed out by new growth — this is a good sign
- Possible side effects: scalp irritation, rarely unwanted facial hair growth
- Discuss with a dermatologist before starting
